How the company started?

1886

Whittard of Chelsea was originally founded in London by Walter Whittard as a commodity and retail trading house for teas from the British colonies. The shop,soon established a reputation for its fine teas by adhering to Whittard's motto: "Buy the Best."

1890-1939

Whittard expanded his shop offerings with the introduction of a selection of coffee blends as well. He also was joined by sons, who later took over operation of the family business after their father's death in 1935.Orders quickly came in from all over the United Kingdom, and even from abroad, as visitors to the United Kingdom began requesting shipments of teas.

1940-1970

The company warehouse and shop are destroyed by bombs, so they moves to Fulham Road in Chelsea. in the early 1970s, when Richard Whittard sold his stake in the family store and business to David Gyle-Thompson Thompson maintained the original store, then added two more London-area stores

1996-1998

Over the next half-decade, Whittard added more than 80 stores, topping 100 in 1996. Although most of these stores were in England, with the majority in the London and southeast regions, Whittard also had gone international, adding stores in France, Poland, Iceland, and Japan. Nonetheless, Whittard pushed ahead with its expansion, building up its number of U.K. stores to nearly 120 by 1998.

2000

Whittard takes over the e-commerce site and decides to transform itself into an Internet company, acquiring bestofbritish.com.

2003

The company opens its first U.S.-based in-store corner shop in the Marshall Field's department store.
